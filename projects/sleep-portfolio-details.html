Project Overview

Introduction: Sleep health is a fundamental pillar of overall well-being, intricately linked to physical health, cognitive function, and emotional equilibrium. The profound impact of adequate and quality sleep on the body's recovery, immune function, mental acuity, and mood regulation underscores its significance in leading a healthy and productive life. This project delves into the critical interplay between sleep patterns, lifestyle factors, and potential sleep disorders to contribute valuable insights to the broader understanding of sleep health.

Dataset Overview: The Sleep Health and Lifestyle Dataset, comprising 400 rows and 13 columns, offers a comprehensive exploration of variables related to sleep and daily habits. Encompassing details such as gender, age, occupation, sleep duration, quality, physical activity levels, stress levels, BMI categories, blood pressure, heart rate, daily steps, and the presence or absence of sleep disorders, the dataset provides a rich source for unraveling the complexities of sleep health.

Purpose: The analysis unfolds through a meticulous examination of sleep metrics, lifestyle factors, and cardiovascular health within the dataset. The chosen tools, Datawrapper and Flourish Studio, facilitate the creation of interactive visualizations that bring to life the intricate relationships between sleep patterns, daily habits, and potential sleep disorders. The ultimate goal is to contribute insights that may inform strategies for enhancing sleep health and overall well-being on both individual and broader public health scales. The story crafted using Flourish provides a visual journey into these relationships, complemented by embedded links to visualizations conducted on Datawrapper.
