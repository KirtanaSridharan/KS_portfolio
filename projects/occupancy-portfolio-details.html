Project Overview

Introduction: In the pursuit of energy-efficient building management, the ability to predict room occupancy plays a pivotal role. This project focuses on utilizing Machine Learning (ML) algorithms to predict room occupancy based on environmental variables. Replacing traditional sensors with cost-effective and maintenance-free ML models enhances the control system's efficiency, particularly in smart building applications like HVAC and lighting systems.

Dataset Overview: The dataset consists of time-stamped observations of environmental variables, including temperature, humidity, light, CO2 levels, and humidity ratio. The dataset is divided into training and testing sets, considering variations in office door status (opened or closed during occupancy). The target variable, Occupancy (0 for not occupied, 1 for occupied), is the focus for prediction. Dataset link

Purpose: The primary objective is to implement ML algorithms for accurate occupancy detection, providing insights into room utilization for building systems optimization. By training models on environmental variables, the project seeks to achieve a reliable and cost-effective alternative to physical sensors, contributing to energy savings and sustainable building practices.

Approach: The project employs two ML algorithms: Neural Networks and Logistic Regression. Neural Networks, with both single and dual-variable configurations, demonstrate exceptional accuracy, reaching 98.84%. Logistic Regression, utilizing one and two variables, yields strong accuracy as well, reaching 98.64%. The project explores the impact of various environmental factors on occupancy prediction.
